Getting Started

Precog Markets is a fully non-custodial prediction market built on Solana. Everything happens on-chain - no accounts, no databases, no middlemen.

Tap Connect in the top-right corner. Phantom, Solflare, Jupiter, and Backpack wallets are supported on desktop. On mobile, the app uses the Solana Mobile Wallet Adapter. If you have a .sol domain, it will display in the wallet button and throughout the app.

Connection Indicator - The small dot next to the wallet button shows your connection status. Grey means no wallet is connected. Green (pulsing) means your wallet is connected and you have full access. Red (pulsing) means your wallet is connected but missing the required token for gated actions - you can still browse and claim, but can't place positions or create markets.

Token Gate - This app may require your wallet to hold a specific token in any amount to place positions and create markets. If your wallet doesn't hold the required token, you'll see a warning banner on the Create Market page and the Choose Position card. The connection indicator will pulse red instead of green. Browsing, viewing markets, and claiming payouts are always open to everyone.

Exploring Markets

The Explore tab is your home screen. Each market card shows outcome probability bars, volume, USD value, position count, time remaining, and maker (with SNS name when available). If you have a position in a market, badges show your estimated profit per outcome and an overall estimated PnL.

Use the Filters button to reveal the filter panel. Your filter and sort selections are saved locally and persist across page reloads.

Status filter - Filter by Open, Closed (pending resolution), Resolved (dispute window active), Ready (awaiting finalization), Finalized, Voided, or All.

Category filter - Filter by the market's on-chain category. Categories are listed alphabetically, with "All Categories" and "Uncategorized" at the top.

Sort - Order by Newest First (default), Oldest First, Closing Soon, Value High/Low, or Most/Least Positions.

Maker filter - Filter by your saved market makers. Save makers from any market detail page using the + button next to the Maker address. Only saved makers appear in this dropdown.

Token filter - Filter by token denomination. Shows the top 5 tokens by open market count with a search field to find any token by name, symbol, or mint address.

My Markets - Toggle to show only markets where you are the authority or maker.

Street Bets - Toggle to show only markets with a short total runtime. These are tagged with an orange "Street Bet" badge.

Market Detail

Tap any market card to open its detail page. You'll see the full description, outcome distribution charts with USD values, pool breakdown, authority and maker addresses (with SNS names), and fee structure.

Save Maker - Next to the Maker address, tap the + button to save that maker to your Saved Market Makers list. The button turns green with a ✓ when saved. Saved makers appear in the Maker filter dropdowns on both Explore and Positions pages. You can manage your saved makers in the Settings tab.

If you hold a position, badges show your estimated profit per outcome. On finalized markets, Won and Lost badges show actual results and a PnL badge shows your total profit or loss across all positions in that market.

Use the Share button to send the market link to others via your device's native share sheet, or it will copy the link to your clipboard.

The detail page updates automatically when you connect or disconnect your wallet, showing or hiding authority actions and position-specific UI as appropriate.

Choosing a Position

On the market detail page (open markets only), select the outcome you want to take a position on, enter your amount, and review the estimated profit with USD value. The estimate updates live based on current pool sizes and the total fee.

For SOL markets, the minimum position is 0.01 SOL. Token markets have no minimum - any amount works depending on the token.

Tap Confirm Position and approve the transaction in your wallet. Your funds go directly into the on-chain vault - not to any company or individual.

Markets can be denominated in native SOL, SPL tokens, or Token-2022 tokens. The denomination is shown on the market card and detail page.

Markets that are past their deadline show as Pending and no longer accept new positions.

If a token gate is active, a warning banner will appear at the top of the Choose Position card telling you which token(s) you need to hold. You can still view the market and its odds - you just can't place a position without the required token.

Market Lifecycle

Open Accepting positions. Anyone can take a position on any outcome.

Pending The deadline has passed but the authority hasn't resolved yet. No new positions accepted. Displayed as "Closed" in the status filter.

Resolved The authority declared a winning outcome. A 24-hour dispute window begins. No new positions. The badge shows "Resolved" while the window is active. Markets with no positions cannot be resolved - use Void instead.

Ready The dispute window has passed. Anyone can trigger finalization. Use the "Ready" status filter to find these markets quickly.

Finalized The dispute window has passed and the market has been finalized. Winners can claim payouts. Finalization is permissionless - anyone can trigger it once the window expires.

Voided The authority cancelled the market. All participants can claim full refunds regardless of outcome.

Dispute Window

After a market is resolved, a 24-hour dispute window begins before the market can be finalized. This gives participants time to review the outcome and gives the authority a chance to correct mistakes.

Change Resolution - During the dispute window, the market authority can change the winning outcome to a different one. This restarts the full 24-hour window so everyone has time to review the updated result.

Void Market - The authority can also void the market during the dispute window, which cancels it entirely and allows all participants to claim full refunds.

Once the 24-hour window passes without any changes, anyone can finalize the market by clicking the Finalize Market button. No special permissions are needed to finalize.

Positions - Filtering & Sorting

The Positions tab shows all your positions across every market. Use the Filters button to reveal the filter panel:

Status - Show only positions in Open, Closed (pending resolution), Resolved (dispute window active), Ready (awaiting finalization), Finalized, or Voided markets.

Category - Filter by the market's on-chain category.

Result - Filter by Unclaimed (positions with a pending Claim Winnings or Claim Refund button), Claimed, Winning (open markets where your outcome is currently leading), Losing (open markets where your outcome is behind), Won (resolved/finalized markets where you hold the winning outcome), or Lost (resolved/finalized markets where you don't).

Sort - Order by newest/oldest (default), deadline, position size, estimated payout, or group by market status.

Maker - Filter by your saved market makers. Only saved makers appear in this dropdown.

Token - Filter by token denomination with search.

My Markets / Street Bets - Toggle buttons to narrow results further.

Each position card shows the outcome, amount, deposit date, deadline, maker, and Street Bet badge where applicable. Profit/loss badges show estimated or actual results, and a PnL badge appears on finalized markets.

If a market is finalized and you hold the winning outcome, a Claim Winnings button appears. For voided markets, you'll see Claim Refund. The app waits for full transaction confirmation before updating.

All filter and sort selections are saved locally and persist across page reloads.

Making a Market

Go to the Make tab. Fill in a title, optional category, description, and define 2-10 outcomes. Set a resolution deadline.

Maker Fee - Optionally set an additional fee (0-500 bps / up to 5%) that you earn on top of the protocol default. This is paid out of winning claims directly to your wallet.

Denomination - Choose Native SOL, SPL Token, or Token-2022. For token markets, paste the mint address and a live preview with the token icon, name, and symbol will appear from on-chain metadata.

The category is stored on-chain inside the description and displayed as a badge on market cards. As the maker, you are the market authority - responsible for resolving with the correct outcome or voiding if needed.

If the protocol admin has paused the system, the Make form will be disabled with a notice. Claims and refunds remain available during a pause.

Watchlist

Tap the ☆ star on any market card to add it to your watchlist. A picker appears with your existing categories and a field to create a new one inline - just type a name and tap +.

In the Watchlist tab, use the Categories button to reveal category tabs for filtering. Reassign categories on individual cards, and manage your categories with the ⚙ button. Categories are sorted alphabetically.

Trash - A built-in protected category that always appears last. Move unwanted markets to Trash instead of removing them. Use the Clear Trash button to permanently remove all trashed markets.

Export / Import - In the category manager (⚙), use Export to save your entire watchlist as a .json file, and Import to merge a saved watchlist back in. Importing adds new markets and categories without duplicating existing ones.

Your watchlist is stored locally in your browser.

Settings

The Settings tab lets you manage your preferences and saved data.

Saved Market Makers

Your saved makers list controls which makers appear in the Maker filter dropdowns on the Explore and Positions pages.

To save a maker, visit any market detail page and tap the + button next to the Maker address. It turns green with a ✓ when saved. Tap again to unsave.

In Settings, you can view all saved makers (with SNS names when available), remove individual makers with the ✕ button, and export/import your list.

Export - Downloads your saved makers as a timestamped .json file.

Import - Merges a saved makers file into your current list without duplicating existing entries.

Both Maker filter dropdowns update immediately whenever you add or remove a maker from any page. Your saved makers list is stored locally in your browser.

Sharing

There are two ways to share. The share icon in the top-right nav bar shares whatever page you're on. On market detail pages, the dedicated Share button includes the market title for richer context.

On mobile, this opens your device's native share sheet. On desktop, it copies the link to your clipboard.

Every market has a permanent deep link: #/market/<address>. Anyone who opens it lands directly on that market.

Fees

Fees are charged on winning claims only - never on losing positions, voided refunds, or market creation.

The total fee is split between the protocol treasury (the default fee set by the admin) and the market maker (any additional fee they set). The fee breakdown is visible on each market's detail page.

Protocol Pause

The protocol admin can pause the system, which prevents new markets from being created and new positions from being placed. Claims and refunds always remain available even when paused - your funds are never locked.

Tips

• Outcome bars show implied probabilities based on pool sizes - they update live as positions come in.

• Profit estimates with USD values appear on market cards, position cards, and the bet input when you have an active position.

• Data refreshes automatically every 20 seconds on the Explore page.

• Priority fee level, compute tolerance, and Street Bet threshold are configurable via environment variables at build time.

• This app has zero backend infrastructure. If the website goes down, your funds are always safe on-chain and recoverable with any Solana program explorer.

Powered by Precog Markets

Precog Markets is built on top of the Precog Markets protocol - a trustless pari-mutuel prediction market program deployed on the Solana blockchain. The protocol handles all market creation, position management, resolution, dispute windows, finalization, and payouts entirely on-chain with no intermediaries.

The protocol supports native SOL, SPL Token, and Token-2022 denominations (including mints with transfer fee extensions), on-chain M-of-N multisig governance for market authorities, and a permissionless finalization model where anyone can crank finalization once the dispute window passes.

The open-source JavaScript SDK that powers this app is available as an npm package for developers who want to build their own interfaces or integrations on the same protocol. The source of the Solana program will be made public soon for those who want to deploy their own protocol from the ground up.
